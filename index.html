ABOUT ME

I am a PhD student in City and Regional Planning at Georgia Institute of Technology and a research assistant at the Center for Spatial Planning Analytics and Visualization (CSPAV) working with Dr. Subhrajit Guhathakurta.
My research interests center around travel behavior — I study (1) the ways (e.g., transportation mode and route) in which people move around urban space, (2) the factors (e.g., built environment, infrastructure, and human activities) that make people choose more walking, biking, and public transit, and (3) how the travel behavior is associated with public health and quality of life through the lens of sustainability + equity.
My dissertation attempts to debunk myths behind "Bikelash" (i.e., the backlash towards cyclists and bike lanes) and to demonstrate how bike lanes can contribute to making a more sustainable and equitable transportation environment.
I also enjoy applying effective visualization techniques and state-of-the-art data analytical tools to urban studies. If you want to get a glimpse of what I do, see Research Highlights below.

Impact of Bike Lanes
on Mode Choice

Making people drive less is the utmost goal in the major U.S. cities. By simulating bike routes of more than 100,000 trips and running a mode choice model, this study demonstrates that bike lanes not only support cyclists but also induce people to shift from driving to walking and public transit.

Covid-19 and
Changes in Eating Habit

Eating behaviors are certainly one of the primary things that the pandemic has affected in negative ways. Using restaurant foot traffic data from SafeGraph, this study shows in what way our eating habits became unhealthier during the pandemic and how the changes differ by neighborhood characteristics.

Exploring
Place Social Diversity

Explore the social diversity among visitors at third places in the City of Atlanta. What types of places let you intermingle with people with diverse backgrounds? The visualization was created by Uijeong Hwang and Xiaofan Liang based on data shared by Seolha Lee.

Atlanta's Transit Equity:
present and future

The on-demand multimodal transit system is designed to connect people to rail/bus networks by serving first/last miles via on-demand shuttles. The novel transit system will surely improve accessibility, but will the improvement be "equitable" across the city?

Algorithm for generating
complete street network

Complete streets aim to ensure safe travel for all street users regardless of their age, ability, or mode of transportation. Due to the inevitable piecemeal nature of those projects, however, complete streets are not complete from the network standpoint. This study investigates the design of a complete street network by using bike lane networks as connective threads.

"Streetscape" as
part of "servicescape"

This study extends the concept of servicescapes to include walkable streetscapes since it contributes to the perceived safety and superior aesthetic values of the street frontage. The analysis shows that the quality of streetscapes (measured through a Computer vision model) have a positive effect on the customer satisfaction (derived from Yelp reviews).
